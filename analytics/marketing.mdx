The **Marketing** category in Analytics reports on how your gym acquires and converts prospects, how your email sends perform, and how your promotions get redeemed. It reads from your deals pipeline, your sent emails, and your promotion redemptions, so the numbers reflect the same records you manage under **Marketing** and **Revenue**.

## Overview

Open **Analytics** in the main nav and select the **Marketing** category to reach three dashboards:

- **Marketing dashboard** (`/analytics/marketing/dashboard`) - sales funnel, lead sources, conversion rates, and pipeline value from your deals.
- **Email analytics dashboard** (`/analytics/marketing/email-dashboard`) - delivery and engagement status for sent emails, plus unsubscribed contacts.
- **Promotions** (`/analytics/marketing/promotions-dashboard`) - redemption volume, credit issued, discounts given, and per-promotion and per-member breakdowns.

All three require the `analytics.read.club` permission. Each dashboard opens on a filter bar: a **Time range** selector and, where it applies, a club selector.

### Filtering

The filter bar at the top of each dashboard controls what the metrics cover:

- **Time range** presets: **Today**, **Yesterday**, **Last 3 days**, **Last 7 days**, **Last 2 weeks**, **Last 30 days**, **Last 90 days**, **Last 6 months**, **Last year**, **This year**, **Previous year**, and **Custom date range**. Choosing **Custom** shows **Start date** and **End date** pickers.
- **Club** filter: on the **Marketing dashboard** you can scope to a single club or leave it on all clubs. The **Email** and **Promotions** dashboards are organization-wide and have no club filter.

Your last-used time range is remembered per dashboard, and preset ranges are reflected in the URL so you can bookmark or share a filtered view.

## Marketing dashboard

This dashboard turns your [deals pipeline](/marketing/pipeline) into funnel and conversion metrics for the selected date range. The date range filters on when each deal was created.

### Summary metrics

Two rows of cards summarize the pipeline:

| Card | What it means |
| --- | --- |
| **Total Leads** | Count of deals created in the range. |
| **Pipeline Value** | Sum of the `value` field across those deals. |
| **Win Rate** | Won deals as a share of decided deals: `won / (won + lost)`. |
| **Average Deal Value** | Pipeline value divided by the number of deals. |
| **Qualified Leads** | Deals whose stage name contains "qualified". |
| **Proposals** | Deals whose stage name contains "proposal". |
| **Closed Won** | Deals whose stage name contains "won". |
| **Closed Lost** | Deals whose stage name contains "lost". |

Qualified, Proposals, Closed Won, and Closed Lost are derived by matching keywords in your **stage names**, not from a fixed status field. If your pipeline stages are not named with those words, these cards and the conversion rates below read 0 even when deals exist. See [Troubleshooting](#troubleshooting).

### Charts

- **Sales Funnel** - a horizontal bar per pipeline stage, ordered by the stage sort order you set in your pipeline, showing the deal count in each stage.
- **Lead Sources** - deal count grouped by [deal source](/marketing/pipeline). Deals with no source are grouped as **Unknown**.
- **Lead Trends** - a line of deals created per day across the range, with empty days filled as 0.
- **Conversion Rates** - four tiles: **Leads to Qualified**, **Qualified to Proposal**, **Proposal to Won**, and **Overall** (won as a share of total leads).

## Email analytics dashboard

This dashboard reports on emails your gym has sent, drawn from your messages and their per-recipient delivery and engagement status. It is organization-wide and filtered only by time range.

### Summary metrics

- **Total Sent** - the count of email recipients in the range.
- A card per status that has activity. The effective status prefers a recipient's engagement status (for example **Opened**, **Clicked**), then delivery status (**Delivered**, **Bounced**, **Dropped**), then the message status. Statuses you may see include **Delivered**, **Opened**, **Clicked**, **Deferred**, **Bounced**, **Dropped**, **Spam Report**, **Unsubscribed**, and **Failed**.

Only statuses with a non-zero count appear, so the cards adapt to what actually happened in the range.

### Charts and tables

- **Sent Emails by Status per Week** - a stacked bar chart with one bar per ISO week, each segment a delivery or engagement status.
- **Unsubscribed Contacts** - a table of contacts who have opted out of marketing email, with their opt-out date. This reads from each contact's marketing email preference, so it also includes people who unsubscribed outside a specific send.

## Promotions

This dashboard measures how your [promotions](/marketing/promotions) (vouchers and discounts) get redeemed. The date range filters on when each redemption happened, not when the promotion was created. Redemptions tied to a voided or refunded transaction are excluded, so the totals reflect settled activity only.

### Summary metrics

| Card | What it means |
| --- | --- |
| **Active Promotions** | Promotions currently within their start/end window, shown as `active / total`. |
| **Total Redemptions** | Count of settled redemptions in the range. |
| **Credit Issued** | Amount credited to member wallets by voucher redemptions. |
| **Discounts Given** | Amount taken off orders at checkout, including vouchers applied as a booking discount rather than wallet credit. |
| **Voucher redemptions** | Redemptions in the range for voucher-type promotions, with the configured voucher count in parentheses. |
| **Discount redemptions** | Redemptions in the range for discount-type promotions, with the configured discount count in parentheses. |

A voucher applied directly as money off a booking counts under **Discounts Given**, not **Credit Issued**, because no wallet was credited. Only a voucher that actually credits a wallet counts as credit issued.

### Charts and tables

- **Redemption Trend** - a dual-axis line chart of redemption **count** and redeemed **amount** per day.
- **Per-Promotion Summary** - one row per promotion with its **code**, **Uses**, **Revenue** (total order value on redemptions), **Discount** (amount given), **New Users** (redeemers who joined within 30 days before redeeming), and **Avg Order**.
- **Per-Member Summary** - one row per member with **Uses**, **Promotions Used** (distinct promotions), **Total Saved/Credited**, and **Last Redemption**. Redemptions with no member are grouped into a single **Anonymous** row.

Both tables are capped at the top 200 rows, and the underlying aggregation reads the most recent 10,000 redemptions, so very high-volume ranges may not include the oldest redemptions.

## Worked examples

**Padel club measuring lead quality by channel.** A padel club runs paid ads, walk-in enquiries, and a website contact form. On the **Marketing dashboard** they set the range to **Last 90 days** and read **Lead Sources** to see which channel produces the most deals, then check **Win Rate** and the **Conversion Rates** tiles to confirm those leads actually convert. Ads may bring the most leads while walk-ins convert at a higher rate.

**Jiu-jitsu academy validating an intro-offer voucher.** The academy issues a "first month free" voucher and wants to know whether it brings in new members rather than rewarding existing ones. On **Promotions**, the **Per-Promotion Summary** row for that voucher shows **New Users** alongside **Uses**, so they can see how many redeemers were genuinely new, and **Revenue** shows the order value those redemptions carried.

**Yoga studio checking a re-engagement email.** After sending a win-back email to lapsed members, the studio opens the **Email analytics dashboard** for the matching week, reads **Delivered** and **Opened** against **Total Sent**, and watches the **Unsubscribed Contacts** table to make sure the campaign is not costing them their list.

## Tips & best practices

- Name your pipeline stages with the words the funnel expects ("Qualified", "Proposal", "Closed Won", "Closed Lost") so the conversion metrics populate. See [Pipeline](/marketing/pipeline).
- Always set a **deal source** on new deals so the **Lead Sources** chart stays meaningful and the **Unknown** bucket stays small.
- Compare like-for-like ranges (for example **This year** versus **Previous year**) rather than mixing a preset with a custom span.
- Read promotion **Revenue** and **Avg Order** together with **Uses**: a promotion with many uses but low revenue may be discounting members who would have paid full price.
- Use the **Unsubscribed Contacts** table as a health check after every large send, not only when open rates drop.

## Troubleshooting

| Symptom | Cause | Fix |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Qualified Leads**, **Proposals**, or the conversion tiles show 0 despite active deals | The metrics match keywords in stage names; your stages are not named "qualified", "proposal", "won", or "lost" | Rename your pipeline stages to include those words, or read the raw **Sales Funnel** counts instead. |
| Many deals fall under a single **Unknown** lead source | Those deals have no deal source set | Set a source on each deal so sources break down correctly. |
| **Credit Issued** looks lower than expected while **Discounts Given** is high | Vouchers applied as money off a booking count as discounts, not wallet credit | This is expected; only wallet-crediting voucher redemptions count as credit issued. |
| Promotion totals seem to miss older redemptions in a wide range | The aggregation reads the most recent 10,000 redemptions | Narrow the date range so the window fits under the cap. |
| A dashboard stays empty | No records match the range, or you lack `analytics.read.club` | Widen the time range; confirm your role grants the analytics read permission. |
| Email counts differ from your send tool | Statuses use the effective per-recipient status (engagement over delivery over message status) | Read the per-status cards together rather than any single number. |
