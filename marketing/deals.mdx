A **deal** is a CRM sales opportunity attached to a contact - a prospect or member you are trying to close on a membership, a class pack, personal training, or any other sale. Each deal sits in a pipeline stage and carries a value, a win probability, a source, and an expected close date, so you can forecast revenue and stay on top of follow-ups. Deals live inside the [Pipeline](/marketing/pipeline) page, which gives you both a Kanban board and a table view of the same records.

## Overview

- Attach an opportunity to any contact and move it through your pipeline stages.
- Track value, win probability, source, expected close date, and an assigned owner per deal.
- Drag deal cards between stages on a Kanban board, or work them from a sortable table.
- Auto-fill probability from each stage's default and override it per deal.
- Require a reason whenever a deal is marked lost, and keep it on the record for reporting.
- Add your own structured fields (custom fields) to capture whatever your sales process needs.
- Configure stages, sources, and custom fields once and apply them to every deal in the organization.

## Deals, pipeline, and promotions

These three are easy to confuse, so keep them straight:

- **Deals** (this page) are CRM opportunities. Each deal belongs to one contact and has a stage, value, source, and assigned owner.
- **[Pipeline](/marketing/pipeline)** is the page where deals live. It has two views of the same records: a **Pipeline** Kanban board grouped by stage, and a **Deals** table. There is no separate "Deals" item in the sidebar - both views sit under **Marketing → Pipeline**.
- **[Promotions](/marketing/promotions)** are vouchers and discount codes you offer to members. They are unrelated to deals - if you are looking for "promo codes" or "discounts," that is the page you want.

## What is on a deal

- **Contact** - the prospect or member this deal is for. Required. Select an existing contact or create a new one inline (new contacts are created as leads). The deal is identified by its contact; there is no separate deal-name field.
- **Stage** - current position in the pipeline. Required. The picker shows each stage's colour and flags **Won** and **Lost** stages.
- **Source** - where the lead came from (optional).
- **Value** - the monetary size of the opportunity, entered in your organization's currency.
- **Probability** - chance of winning, 0-100%. Auto-filled from the stage's default probability and overridable per deal.
- **Expected close date** - when you expect the deal to close.
- **Assigned to** - the team member responsible for working the deal. Defaults to you when you create it; you can pick another admin user or leave it **Unassigned**.
- **Notes** - free-form context.
- **Close lost reason** - required when you move the deal into a lost stage; stored on the deal and shown on its card.
- **Custom fields** - any extra fields you have configured (see [Configuring deals](#configuring-deals)).

## Creating a deal

1. Go to **Marketing → Pipeline** and click **Add deal** - or open a contact's profile, go to the **Deals** tab, and add a deal there (the contact is pre-selected).
2. Choose the **contact**: keep the **Select existing** toggle to search your contacts, or switch to **Create** to add a new contact (first name, last name, email) on the spot.
3. Pick the starting **stage** (required). The deal's **probability** fills in from that stage's default; adjust it if needed.
4. Fill in **source**, **value**, **expected close date**, **assigned to**, and **notes** as needed.
5. **Save.**

Creating a deal automatically re-evaluates the contact's type, so a plain lead shows up correctly in your member lists and reports once they have an active opportunity.

## Working the pipeline

Open **Marketing → Pipeline** and use the **Pipeline / Deals** toggle at the top to switch views:

- **Pipeline** is the Kanban board. Each column is a stage (coloured by its stage colour) and shows a deal count and total value. Drag a deal card from one column to another to change its stage. When you drop a deal, its probability auto-updates to the new stage's default probability. Click a card to edit the deal, or click an empty part of a column to add a deal that starts in that stage.
- **Deals** is a sortable table with columns for contact, stage, source, value, probability, expected close date, assigned owner, and created date - handy for bulk review. Clicking a row opens that contact's profile on the **Deals** tab.

The board and the table are two views of the same deal records, so a change in one shows up in the other.

## Closing won or lost

Every stage has a **type**: **Open**, **Won**, or **Lost**. When you move a deal:

- **To a Won stage** - the deal closes as won and its value counts toward your won totals.
- **To a Lost stage** - you must enter a **Close lost reason** before the move is accepted. The reason is stored on the deal and shown on its card for later review.

You can move a deal back out of a Won or Lost stage to an Open stage if circumstances change.

## Configuring deals

Pipeline configuration lives under **Settings → CRM → Deals**, split into three tabs.

### Stages

Stages are the columns on your pipeline board. For each stage you set:

- **Name** and **colour** (the colour tints the board column and deal chips).
- **Sort order** - the left-to-right order of columns; reorder stages to match your process.
- **Type** - **Open**, **Won**, or **Lost**. Type drives closing behaviour (won totals, the mandatory lost reason).
- **Default probability** (0-100) - auto-applied to any deal that lands in this stage.
- **Default** - marks the stage new deals start in.

Use **Reset to default** to restore the standard set. The default stages are:

| Stage | Type | Default probability |
| --- | --- | --- |
| New Deal | Open | 10% |
| Qualified | Open | 50% |
| Negotiation | Open | 70% |
| Proposal Sent | Open | 90% |
| Closed Won | Won | 100% |
| Closed Lost | Lost | 0% |

You cannot delete a stage that still has deals in it - move those deals to another stage first.

### Sources

Sources are the lead-origin options you choose from when creating a deal. Each source has a name, an optional description, a colour, and a sort order. Use **Reset to default** to restore the standard set: **Website**, **Referral**, **Cold Call**, **Social Media**, **Email Campaign**, **Trade Show**, **Advertisement**, and **Other**.

### Fields (custom fields)

Add structured fields specific to your sales process. Each field has a **name** (internal key), a **label** (what staff see), a **type**, an optional **placeholder** and **default value**, and a **required** toggle. Available field types are text, textarea, number, email, phone, date, select, multi-select, checkbox, and radio. Configured fields appear on every deal's edit dialog.

Configure stages, sources, and fields once and they apply to every deal across the organization.

## Assignment and follow-up

Set an **assigned to** owner on each deal so it is clear who is responsible; it defaults to whoever creates the deal. Use the **Deals** table view to sort and scan by owner, stage, or expected close date, and to spot deals that are unassigned or drifting past their close date.

## Reporting

The **Pipeline** view shows four summary cards across the top:

- **Total deals** - count of deals on the board.
- **Total value** - sum of all deal values.
- **Average probability** - mean win probability across deals.
- **This month** - deals created in the current calendar month.

For deeper analysis, see [Marketing analytics](/analytics/marketing).

## Worked examples

**Racket sports - membership upgrade.** A padel member asks about switching to an unlimited plan. Create a deal on their contact, set the **stage** to *Qualified*, **source** to *Referral*, **value** to the annual plan price, and an **expected close date** two weeks out. As they commit, drag the card to *Proposal Sent*, then to *Closed Won* once they buy - the value lands in your won totals.

**Martial arts - corporate group inquiry.** A local company wants a block of jiu-jitsu intro sessions for their team. Create the deal from the primary contact, set **source** to *Website* (they came through the contact form), and add a custom **select** field "Team size" so every corporate deal captures headcount consistently. Work it across your stages and record a **Close lost reason** if they go with a competitor.

**Pilates/Yoga - trial-to-membership.** A reformer trial-goer is deciding on a package. Open the contact, add a deal at *New Deal* with a modest **probability**, and assign it to the instructor who ran the trial. Move it to *Negotiation* when they ask about pricing, and to *Closed Won* when they sign up.

**Gym/Fitness - reactivation.** A lapsed strength member reaches out. Create a deal, tag the **source** as *Email Campaign* (they clicked a win-back email), and set a near-term expected close date so it surfaces in your follow-up queue.

## Tips and best practices

- **Match your stages to how you actually sell.** Rename, recolour, and reorder stages so the board mirrors your real process rather than a generic funnel.
- **Set honest default probabilities.** They power your average-probability card and give a quick read on pipeline health - keep later stages higher and closed-lost at 0%.
- **Always capture a close lost reason.** It is required on lost moves for a reason: the aggregated reasons tell you why deals slip away.
- **Use custom fields for anything you filter or report on.** A structured "Team size" or "Preferred sport" field beats burying the same detail in free-text notes.
- **Assign every deal an owner.** Unassigned deals are the ones that go quiet - the default-to-creator behaviour helps, but reassign when someone else takes over.

## Troubleshooting

- **"Cannot delete stage with existing deals."** The stage still has deals in it. Move those deals to another stage (drag them on the board or edit each deal), then delete the empty stage.
- **A deal will not save without a lost reason.** You moved it into a **Lost**-type stage; enter a **Close lost reason** to complete the move.
- **Probability keeps changing when I move a deal.** That is by design - dropping a deal into a stage applies that stage's default probability. Override it on the deal afterward if you need a custom figure.
- **I cannot find a "Deals" menu item.** There is not one. Deals live under **Marketing → Pipeline**; use the **Pipeline / Deals** toggle to switch between the board and the table.
- **My source or custom field is not in the dropdown.** Add or re-activate it under **Settings → CRM → Deals** (Sources or Fields tab). Inactive entries do not appear when creating a deal.
