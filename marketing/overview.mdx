**In a nutshell:** Use Marketing to audit your online presence, manage your sales pipeline and deals, run vouchers and discount promotions, and reach members with AI-assisted campaigns.

## Sections

- **[Business audit](/marketing/business-audit)** — Generate AI-powered audits of your online business profile, review sentiment, and website performance.
- **[Pipeline](/marketing/pipeline)** — Kanban view of your sales opportunities by stage.
- **[Deals](/marketing/deals)** — Track sales opportunities (CRM) through your pipeline from lead to close.
- **[Promotions](/marketing/promotions)** — Create vouchers and discounts with codes, schedules, audience targeting and usage caps.
- **[Campaigns](/marketing/campaigns)** — Build and run AI-assisted marketing campaigns (e.g. email) with targeting and reporting.

## Sales pipeline

The Pipeline and Deals pages are two views of the same underlying CRM records — a Kanban board grouped by stage, and a sortable table — so you can work opportunities in whichever way fits the moment.

- **Track sales opportunities** and potential members from first touch to close.
- **Manage leads** from referrals, walk-ins, the website, social, ads, and events.
- **Move deals between stages** with drag-and-drop on the pipeline board.
- **Close as Won or Lost** with a required reason on lost deals for accurate reporting.
- **Forecast** using deal value, win probability, and expected close dates.

Configure stages, sources, and custom fields under **Settings → Deals**.

## Promotions

Promotions cover both **vouchers** (wallet credit redeemed by code) and **discounts** (percentage or fixed amount applied at checkout). See the [Promotions page](/marketing/promotions) for the full reference. Use them to:

- **Acquire new members** with a public code on your signup page.
- **Re-engage lapsed members** with a member-only voucher targeted by tag.
- **Fill specific inventory** by scoping a discount to particular class, area or instructor types.
- **Run time-bound campaigns** with start and end dates and global or per-member redemption caps.

Promotions are **separate from deals** — promotions are codes and offers; deals are sales opportunities.

## Getting started

### Setting up marketing

1. Go to **Marketing → Overview**.
2. **Review your current** marketing setup.
3. **Configure basic settings:**
   - **Brand guidelines** — set up brand standards.
   - **Contact information** — marketing contact details.
   - **Campaign templates** — create email templates.
   - **Social media** — connect social media accounts.
4. **Set up tracking** and analytics.
5. **Begin creating** your first campaign or promotion.

## Sales pipeline management

### Lead sources

- **Website inquiries** — leads from your website.
- **Social media** — leads from social platforms.
- **Referrals** — member referrals.
- **Walk-ins** — in-person inquiries.
- **Events** — leads from events.
- **Advertising** — leads from paid advertising.

Configure the list of sources under **Settings → Deals → Sources**.

### Pipeline stages

Default stages typically look like:

- **Lead** — initial inquiry or interest.
- **Qualified** — qualified potential member.
- **Proposal** — proposal sent to prospect.
- **Negotiation** — negotiating terms.
- **Closed Won** — successfully converted to member.
- **Closed Lost** — did not convert; requires a close-lost reason.

You can rename, reorder, recolour, and add or remove stages under **Settings → Deals → Stages**.

## Analytics and reporting

### Marketing metrics

- **Lead generation** — number of new leads.
- **Conversion rates** — lead-to-member conversion.
- **Cost per lead** — cost to acquire each lead.
- **Customer acquisition cost** — total cost to acquire members.
- **Return on investment** — marketing ROI.
- **Lifetime value** — member lifetime value.

### Campaign performance

- **Email performance** — open rates, click rates.
- **Social media metrics** — engagement, reach, impressions.
- **Website analytics** — traffic, conversions, bounce rate.
- **Campaign ROI** — return on campaign investment.
- **A/B test results** — test performance comparison.
- **Trend analysis** — performance over time.

### Promotion performance

- **Redemptions** — total and per-promotion redemption counts.
- **Credit issued** — total wallet credit from voucher redemptions.
- **Discount given** — total amount discounted at checkout.
- **Revenue impact** — bookings and orders attributable to a promotion.
- **Members acquired** — new members whose first purchase used a promotion.

See [Promotions](/marketing/promotions) for the full breakdown, or the [Marketing analytics](/analytics/marketing) dashboard for cross-feature reporting.

## Best practices

### Marketing strategy

- **Know your audience** — understand your target market.
- **Communicate value clearly** — what makes your club special?
- **Maintain consistent branding** — use a consistent brand image.
- **Use multiple channels** — don't rely on just one marketing method.

### Campaign management

- **Plan campaigns in advance** — don't wait until the last minute.
- **Test and optimize** — continuously improve your campaigns.
- **Measure results** — track and measure performance.
- **Learn from data** — use data to improve future campaigns.

### Member retention

- **Focus on experience** — prioritise member experience.
- **Listen to feedback** — act on member feedback.
- **Reward loyalty** — recognise and reward loyal members.
- **Always improve** — look for ways to make things better.

## Troubleshooting

### Common issues

- **Low conversion** — improve conversion rates with better offers and a clearer pipeline process.
- **High churn** — reduce member churn with retention promotions and engagement campaigns.
- **Poor engagement** — increase member engagement with better content.
- **Low ROI** — improve marketing ROI with better targeting.

### Resolution procedures

- **Analyze performance data** to identify problems.
- **Adjust marketing strategy** based on findings.
- **Improve marketing processes** to prevent issues.
- **Train marketing team** on best practices.
