The Marketing section is where you turn interest into members and existing members into repeat business. It groups five separate tools - each one is a standalone feature; you don't have to use them in order.

## What's in here

- **[Business audit](/marketing/business-audit)** - AI-generated audits of your online business profile, review sentiment, and website performance.
- **[Pipeline](/marketing/pipeline)** - Kanban board of sales opportunities (deals) grouped by stage.
- **[Deals](/marketing/deals)** - The same deals as the Pipeline, but as a sortable table.
- **[Promotions](/marketing/promotions)** - Vouchers (wallet credit redeemed by code) and discounts (percentage or fixed amount at checkout).
- **[Campaigns](/marketing/campaigns)** - AI-assisted marketing campaigns with audience targeting and performance reporting.

## How the pieces fit together

The Pipeline and Deals pages are two views of the same records. Use the Kanban when you're working a list of opportunities and want to drag them between stages; use the table when you're reporting or bulk-editing. Stages, sources, and custom fields are configured once under **Settings > Deals**.

Promotions are separate from deals - promotions are codes and offers, deals are opportunities. A promotion targeting "new signups" might generate many leads that become deals, but the two are linked only loosely through reporting.

Campaigns sit on top of both. A campaign can include a promotion (e.g. "send a 20% voucher to lapsed members tagged `lapsed`") and the resulting bookings or signups show up in the campaign's reports.

## Pipeline at a glance

The default deal stages typically look like Lead, Qualified, Proposal, Negotiation, Closed Won, Closed Lost - but stages, colors, and order are fully configurable under **Settings > Deals > Stages**. Moving a deal into any stage marked as type `lost` prompts you for a close-lost reason so the reporting stays clean.

Sources (where the lead came from - website, referral, walk-in, social, paid ads, events, etc.) are configured separately under **Settings > Deals > Sources**.

For the full reference, see the [Pipeline](/marketing/pipeline) page.

## Promotions at a glance

Promotions cover both **vouchers** and **discounts**:

- **Vouchers** issue wallet credit when a code is redeemed. Members spend the credit on bookings, memberships, or POS.
- **Discounts** apply a percentage or fixed amount at checkout. They can be scoped to specific class types, area types, or instructor types.

Both support audience targeting (tags, contact type), date windows, per-member and global redemption caps, and public versus member-only access. See [Promotions](/marketing/promotions) for the full breakdown.

## Campaigns at a glance

Campaigns let you target a segment, write the message with AI assistance, and send it through email or other channels. Performance (opens, clicks, conversions back to bookings or signups) is tracked per campaign. See [Campaigns](/marketing/campaigns).

## Cross-feature reporting

For metrics across all marketing tools - lead-to-member conversion, campaign ROI, promotion redemptions - use the [Marketing analytics](/analytics/marketing) dashboard. Promotion-specific performance also surfaces on the promotion's own page.

## Getting started

1. Configure pipeline stages and lead sources under **Settings > Deals**.
2. Create your first promotion (a welcome voucher is the usual starting point).
3. Add a few deals manually or import them, then drag them through stages as they progress.
4. Launch a campaign that puts the promotion in front of the right segment.
5. Review marketing analytics monthly and adjust.
