The kiosk is a self-service touchscreen you run at your front desk. Members walk up and check themselves in, mark attendance for a class, buy a membership or day pass, or enroll a check-in credential - without tying up staff. It runs full-screen in a browser on a touchscreen, scoped to a single club. Enable and configure it under **Settings > Kiosk app**.

## Setting it up

The first time you open the kiosk on a device, a one-time setup signs in and picks the organization and club it serves. After that the kiosk remembers its club and boots straight to the home screen. You choose which tiles appear, so a location that only needs check-in can hide the rest.

## What members can do

The home screen shows large touch tiles. Depending on what you enable:

- **Check in** - the primary use. A member identifies themselves and the kiosk records the visit.
- **Classes** - browse the schedule and check in to a specific class.
- **Plans** - browse memberships, and buy one on the spot.
- **Areas** - view facilities and buy a drop-in where you allow it.
- **Products** - buy day passes or other items from your catalog.
- **Enroll credential** - register a QR code, card, or PIN so the member can check in quickly next time.

## Checking in

The kiosk supports the identification methods you have enabled under [Access control](/settings/access-control):

- **Scan a QR code** - the member shows the QR from their [mobile app](/member-experience/mobile-app) card screen.
- **Enter a PIN** - the member types their PIN on the on-screen keypad.
- **Search by name** - the member (or a staff member) looks them up.

The kiosk confirms the match, records the check-in, and shows a brief welcome before returning to the home screen. What happens next - whether a valid membership or payment is required to complete entry - follows your [check-in model](/reference/check-in-models). Check-ins recorded here appear in [Attendance](/members/attendance) like any other.

## Buying at the kiosk

When you enable the plans, areas, or products tiles, a member can complete a purchase at the kiosk and pay by card. This is handy for drop-ins and day passes: a first-time visitor can buy a pass and check in without an account. Purchases flow into your billing the same way as any other sale - see [Billing overview](/billing/overview).

## Hardware

The kiosk is a web app, so it runs on most touchscreen setups - a tablet or a screen driven by a small PC - with the browser in full-screen (kiosk) mode. For QR check-in you can use the device camera or a connected barcode scanner. A stable network connection is required.
