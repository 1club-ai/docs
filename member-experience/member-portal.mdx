title: Member portal
description: The web app where your members sign in to book, buy, pay, sign documents, and manage their account. Branded to your gym and served on your own domain.
---

The member portal is your gym's self-service web app. Members open it in a browser, sign in, and handle everything themselves: booking classes and facilities, buying and managing memberships, paying, signing waivers, and updating their profile. It carries your [branding](/settings/overview) and runs on your own subdomain (or a custom domain), so it feels like part of your gym rather than a generic tool.

Turn the portal on and set its address under **Settings > Member portal**. Until it has a public host, links that depend on it (like [signup form](/settings/signup-forms) URLs) have nowhere to point.

## Getting in

Before a member can use the portal they create an account and clear any required documents.

<Steps>
<Step title="Sign up or sign in">
Members register with email and password, or with a social login (Google, Facebook, or Apple). Existing members sign in the same way. You can also send people through a [signup form](/settings/signup-forms), which onboards them and can attach a plan in one flow.
</Step>
<Step title="Verify email">
When email verification applies, the member confirms their address before continuing.
</Step>
<Step title="Accept documents">
If your [document rules](/content/document-signatures) require waivers, terms, or policies, the member must read and sign them before their account is fully active. This gate also appears when you update a document and ask members to re-sign.
</Step>
</Steps>

Members who forget their password can reset it, and people invited to a [household or company account](/members/accounts) accept the invitation from an emailed link. Members on a [waitlist](/operations/classes) can claim their spot when one opens up.

## Dashboard

Signing in lands the member on their dashboard - a snapshot of their activity:

- **Upcoming bookings**, each with the option to cancel (subject to your cancellation policy).
- **Active memberships** and their status.
- **Wallet balance** and recent activity, when they have credit.
- Quick actions to **book** or browse the schedule.

For multi-location gyms, the dashboard can show a map of your clubs.

## Browsing and booking

Members can browse your offerings before they ever sign in; they only need an account to actually book.

- **Schedule and calendar** - the class timetable and a combined booking calendar across areas and instructors.
- **Classes** - filter by class type, sport, or instructor; open a class to see times, capacity, the instructor, and reviews.
- **Areas** - bookable spaces and facilities, with amenities, availability, and photos.
- **Instructors** - profiles with bios, specialties, and their upcoming sessions.

### The booking flow

When a member books, the portal walks them through picking a time slot, choosing who the booking is for (themselves or a [family member on their account](/members/accounts)), and paying if payment is due. Along the way it shows:

- **Eligibility** - whether an active membership or plan covers the booking, or whether it needs to be paid for.
- **Price** - the amount due after any membership benefit, [promotion](/marketing/promotions), or wallet credit.
- **Waitlist** - if a class is full, the member can join the waitlist and is offered the spot automatically if one frees up.

Whether payment is required up front, at check-in, or not at all is set by the [booking policy](/operations/booking-policies) that applies to the booking. Cancellations follow the refund tiers on that same policy. See [How members pay](/billing/member-payments) for the checkout itself.

## Plans and memberships

Under the plans area, members see the [plans](/billing/plans) you have published to the portal. They can open a plan to read its price, what it covers, and its renewal terms, then join. Buying a plan behaves the same as any membership checkout: if the plan needs payment up front, the member pays before it activates.

From **My account**, members manage what they already hold: active and past memberships, renewal dates, and usage against any plan limits.

## My account

The account area is where members manage their own record:

- **Profile** - name, contact details, date of birth, profile photo, preferred language, and light or dark theme. Which fields appear (and which are required) is driven by your contact-field configuration.
- **Memberships** - active and expired memberships with renewal and usage detail.
- **Bookings** - full history, with cancellation where the policy allows.
- **Billing** - saved cards, payment history, invoices to download, and wallet balance. See [How members pay](/billing/member-payments).
- **Documents** - previously signed documents, plus anything awaiting a new signature.

Members can also delete their own account from here.

## Family members

A member whose account is part of a [household or company](/members/accounts) can act for the people they are allowed to. A household owner or adult can book and pay for a dependent; a company admin can do the same for employees. When booking, the member picks who the booking is for. Billing rolls up to the account.

## Documents and signatures

Beyond the signup gate, members can view the documents they have signed and complete any that are outstanding. The signable documents and the rules for who must sign them are defined under **Settings > Document rules**; the resulting records show up for staff under [Document signatures](/content/document-signatures).

## Content: posts, guides, and FAQs

If you publish them, the portal surfaces content from your CMS:

- **Posts / news** - announcements and articles.
- **How-to guides** - instructional content.
- **FAQs** - answers to common questions.

These come from your [content items](/content/documents) and follow the member's language.

## Inbox

Members have an inbox for messages with your gym. They can read messages you send and reply, and the portal notifies them about booking changes, payment issues, and document requests. On your side, these threads appear in the [messaging](/messaging/overview) inbox.

## Multiple clubs, languages, and theme

- **Multiple locations** - for a multi-club organization, members can view a specific club or an "all clubs" view that combines schedules and bookings across locations. Single-club gyms have a simpler, location-free navigation.
- **Languages** - the portal displays in the member's language when you publish that language, and content follows suit.
- **Theme** - members can switch between light and dark. Colors, logo, and fonts all come from your [branding](/settings/overview).
