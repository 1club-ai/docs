The mobile app gives members the portal experience in their pocket. They install it from the App Store or Google Play, sign in to your gym, and get the same account, memberships, and bookings they have on the web - plus a QR card for check-in and push notifications. Enable and configure it under **Settings > Mobile app**.

## Signing in and getting set up

Members sign in with email and password or a social login (Google, Apple, or Facebook). On first launch they select your organization (and club, if you have more than one). If your [document rules](/content/document-signatures) require it, the app shows the waivers and policies to accept before the member can continue - the same gate as the portal.

## The four tabs

The app is organized into four tabs along the bottom:

- **Today** - the home screen. Upcoming bookings at a glance and quick actions to get moving.
- **Book** - a weekly calendar of classes, areas, and instructors. Members filter, open a slot for detail, book, or join a waitlist.
- **Purchase** - the plan and membership catalog. Members review a plan and buy it in-app; free plans activate immediately.
- **Inbox** - messages from your gym, with an unread badge. Members read and reply, and the badge stays in sync as they catch up.

## Account

Behind a **My account** menu, members manage the same things they can on the portal:

- **Profile** - personal details, photo, and contact information.
- **Bookings** - upcoming and past bookings, with cancellation where your policy allows.
- **Memberships** - active memberships, renewal dates, and usage.
- **Card** - the member's check-in credential (see below).
- **Language** and **Theme** - switch language, or toggle light and dark mode.
- **Delete account** - remove their account and data.

## The QR card and check-in

The **Card** screen shows the member's check-in credential - a QR code (and PIN, when your gym uses one). Members present it to a [kiosk](/member-experience/kiosk) or to front-desk staff to check in. Which credential types your gym accepts is set under [Access control](/settings/access-control), and the credentials themselves are managed on the [Access cards](/members/member-cards) page.

## Paying in the app

Members pay for bookings and plans in-app with a card (and Apple Pay or Google Pay where supported), a saved card, or [wallet](/billing/member-payments) credit. The rules for when payment is due are the same [booking policies](/operations/booking-policies) that apply everywhere else. See [How members pay](/billing/member-payments) for details.

## Push notifications

With the member's permission, the app sends push notifications for things like booking confirmations, class changes, messages, and membership renewals. Tapping a notification opens the relevant screen. The app icon badge tracks unread messages.

## App vs. portal

The app and the [member portal](/member-experience/member-portal) share one account and cover the same core actions, so members can move between them freely. Reach for the app when you want push notifications and a fast, always-with-you QR card; the portal is handy on a laptop and is what public links (signup forms, payment links) open.
