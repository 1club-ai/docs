The corporate admin is a separate, self-serve dashboard for the businesses that sponsor memberships at your gym - the [company accounts](/members/accounts) you set up. Instead of asking your staff to add or remove every employee, a company's own administrator (an HR or office manager) signs in to their corporate dashboard and manages their roster themselves. It is a different app from your gym's admin dashboard, with a narrower, company-scoped view.

<Note>
This is for your B2B clients, not for your gym staff. Your team continues to run everything from the main admin. Individual households do not use the corporate admin - see [Accounts](/members/accounts) for how households and companies differ.
</Note>

## Who uses it

The administrator of a [company account](/members/accounts) - typically the HR contact or office manager at an employer that covers memberships as a benefit. They get their own sign-in, separate from your gym admin and from the member portal.

## What a corporate admin can do

- **Onboard** - complete an initial setup for the company when they first join.
- **Manage employees** - add employees to the account, view and edit their details, and remove people who leave. Each employee becomes a member covered by the company.
- **View assigned clubs** - see the gym locations the company's benefit applies to.
- **Manage company settings** - the company profile and organization details.
- **Sign in and recover access** - their own login, email verification, and password reset.

As the corporate admin manages the roster, the changes flow into your gym's records: employees show up as [members](/members/member-list) on the [company account](/members/accounts), and billing continues to roll up to the account and its billing email.

## How it relates to accounts

You still create and configure the company account itself in your admin - see [Accounts](/members/accounts) for setting up a company, its billing details, and roles. The corporate admin simply hands day-to-day roster management to the client, so your staff are not the bottleneck for a large employer's changes.
