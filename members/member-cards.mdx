In a nutshell, the access cards system lets you manage physical credentials for your members — RFID cards, NFC tags, PINs, and similar — track assignments, and monitor credential status across your organization.

## Overview

The access cards page is the central place to issue and manage physical entry credentials for your members. You can enroll new credentials, assign them to members, revoke or replace lost ones, and review assignment history.

Key capabilities:

- **Credential enrollment**: Issue cards, NFC tags, or PINs for members and staff
- **Member assignment**: Link a credential to a specific member, with primary/secondary designation
- **Status tracking**: Active, revoked, lost, or stolen — with audit history
- **Multiple credential types**: RFID 125kHz, RFID 13.56MHz, NFC, PIN, and other types supported by your access-control configuration
- **Revoke and replace**: Disable a lost credential and issue a replacement in one flow

## Managing Access Cards

### Viewing all credentials

1. Navigate to **Members → Access cards** in the admin panel
2. The grid displays every credential issued for your organization
3. Use search and filters to narrow the list

### Understanding the grid

The grid shows:

- **Identifier**: Card number, tag UID, or PIN reference
- **Type**: Credential type (RFID 125kHz, RFID 13.56MHz, NFC, PIN, etc.)
- **Status**: Active, revoked, lost, or stolen
- **Assigned to**: Member name, or "Unassigned"
- **Issued date**: When the credential was enrolled
- **Notes**: Free-form notes about the credential
- **Actions**: Assign, revoke, or delete (depending on state)

### Enrolling a new credential

1. Click **"Add"** in the top right
2. Choose the credential type
3. Enter the identifier (scan or type the card/tag UID, or set the PIN)
4. Optionally pre-assign it to a member
5. Add notes if needed
6. Save

<Note>
Credential enrollment is gated by your access-control settings. If enrollment is disabled at the organization level, the **Add** button will be hidden. See [Access control](/settings/access-control).
</Note>

### Credential statuses

- **Active**: In use and accepted by access readers
- **Revoked**: Permanently disabled (e.g., member left)
- **Lost / Stolen**: Disabled and flagged in history for follow-up

## Assigning credentials to members

### Assigning

1. Find an unassigned credential in the grid
2. Click the **Assign** action
3. Pick the member
4. Choose whether this is their primary credential
5. Save

### Primary vs secondary

- **Primary**: The member's main credential; only one per member
- **Secondary**: Additional credentials linked to the same member (e.g., a backup tag)

### From a member profile

1. Open **Members → Member list** and click the member
2. The credentials section lists every credential assigned to them, with assignment date and status
3. You can revoke or unassign from there

## Revoking and replacing

### Revoke

1. In the grid (or member profile), click **Revoke** on the credential
2. Optionally choose to revoke all assignments at once
3. The credential is marked revoked and rejected by readers immediately

### Replace a lost or stolen credential

1. Mark the old credential as **Lost** or **Stolen**
2. Enroll a new credential
3. Assign the new credential to the member, marking it primary if needed

### Deleting

- Only **unassigned** credentials can be deleted
- Once a credential has been assigned (even if later unassigned), keep it for audit history

## Common tasks

### Issuing a credential to a new member

1. Enroll the credential under the chosen type
2. Assign it to the member as primary
3. Add notes if relevant (e.g., locker or gate scope)

### Handling a lost card

1. Mark the lost card as **Lost** so readers stop accepting it
2. Enroll a replacement credential
3. Assign the replacement to the member as primary

### Guest or temporary access

1. Enroll a credential intended for short-term use
2. Assign it to the guest contact
3. Revoke it as soon as access is no longer needed

## Tips & best practices

- **Unique identifiers**: Each credential's identifier must be unique within your organization
- **Regular audits**: Periodically review active credentials and revoke ones that should no longer have access
- **Status hygiene**: Always mark lost or stolen credentials immediately — don't just unassign
- **Notes**: Capture context (which gate, which locker, why a secondary credential was issued)
- **Primary designation**: Keep one primary per member so reports and member-facing surfaces show the right card

## Troubleshooting

**Issue**: Cannot enroll a new credential
**Solution**: Check that credential enrollment is enabled in [Access control settings](/settings/access-control), and that your role has permission to manage credentials.

**Issue**: Identifier already exists
**Solution**: Each credential identifier must be unique. Check the existing record and revoke or delete it before reusing the value.

**Issue**: Cannot delete a credential
**Solution**: Credentials with assignment history can be revoked but not deleted. This preserves the audit trail.

**Issue**: Member has multiple primary credentials
**Solution**: Only one credential can be primary per member. Setting a new primary automatically demotes the old one.
