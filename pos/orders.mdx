An order is the checkout record for a single sale. It groups everything bought together - bookings, memberships, products, packages, and any discount - as line items, then executes them into real records and settles payment.

Orders are the shared checkout for the whole platform. Whether the sale is rung up at the front desk on the [point of sale](/pos/point-of-sale), placed by a member in the portal or app, or completed on your website, it becomes an order and flows through the same lifecycle.

## Opening the page

Go to **Billing > Orders** in the main menu. The URL is `/billing/orders`.

Tabs filter by status, each with a live count:

- **All** - every order.
- **Draft** - started but not yet executed.
- **Completed** - executed and settled.
- **Cancelled** - discarded or voided.

Each row shows the order number, the member, its status, payment status, item count, total, and when it was created. Click a row to open the order, or use the **POS** button to jump to the register. From a row you can view the order or cancel it (unless it is already cancelled).

## Line items

Every order is a list of line items. Each item has a type:

- **Booking** - a class, area, instructor, or event registration.
- **Membership** - a plan assigned to the member.
- **Membership signup fee** - the one-time fee that accompanies a membership, recorded as its own line.
- **Product** - a retail [product](/pos/products), with a quantity.
- **Package** - a fixed-price [package](/billing/packages). Its bundled plans and products appear as child lines beneath it.
- **Discount** - a [promotion](/marketing/promotions) applied at checkout, recorded as a negative line so the order total nets out correctly.

The order detail page lists every line with its type, description, quantity, unit price, and total (with a tax breakdown where tax applies). Once an order is executed, each line links to what it created - the booking, membership, or [transaction](/billing/transactions) - so you can trace a sale end to end.

## Order statuses

An order moves through three states:

- **Draft** - the order is being built. Its items are not yet real bookings or memberships. Drafts can be edited, resumed on the POS, completed, or discarded.
- **Completed** - the order has been executed. Its line items now exist as bookings, memberships, and product transactions.
- **Cancelled** - the order was discarded or cancelled and will not be executed.

## How an order becomes a sale

Orders follow the same path regardless of where they start:

1. **Create** - a draft order is created with its line items. On the POS this happens as you build the cart; in the portal, app, and website it is created at checkout.
2. **Execute** - the order is executed. Each line becomes a real record: bookings are created, memberships are assigned, and product sales are written as transactions. Any promotion is applied to the eligible line at this point.
3. **Settle payment** - payment is collected and recorded against the generated transactions. At the front desk the operator records the tender; in the portal, app, and website the member pays through Stripe. When the balance is cleared, the order is complete.

Because execution and payment are separate steps, an order can legitimately be completed with an outstanding balance (for example a booking that is paid at check-in), or settled in full up front.

### Completing a draft

Open a draft order and click **Complete order** to execute and settle it from the order detail page, the same way the POS does.

## Booker versus attendee

Most orders are for the member who is paying. Some are placed on behalf of someone else - for example a parent booking for a child, or one member paying for a household.

Every order records two contacts:

- The **attendee** - who the booking, membership, or product is for.
- The **booker** - who pays. The booker owns the wallet debits, refunds, and the card used to charge the order.

When the booker and attendee are the same person - the common case - only the attendee is stored and the booker is left blank.

## External settlement and integrations

When an order is paid by card, it tracks the payment intent (Stripe) or terminal reference (for example myPOS) used to settle it. If an order has been synced to an external system - for example a POS used for fiscalization - the order detail page shows an **Integrations** panel listing each linked system and when it was synced.
