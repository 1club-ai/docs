1club supports **Sign in with Google**, **Sign in with Apple**, and **Facebook login**, plus classic **email and password**, across all of its apps. Members, staff, and admins can use whichever method they prefer, and the same identity works on the web and in the mobile app.

## Overview

- **Four ways to sign in** - Google, Apple, Facebook, or email and password.
- **One identity everywhere** - the same account works on the admin dashboard, member portal, marketplace, club website, and mobile app.
- **One email, one account** - an account is keyed to its email address, no matter which method created it.
- **Automatic account creation** - signing in with a social provider for the first time creates a 1club account or links to the matching one.
- **Self-service and admin-assisted recovery** - members reset their own password; staff who signed up with a social provider can have a password enabled by an admin.
- **Private email support** - Apple's Hide My Email works end to end, including notifications.

## Supported sign-in methods

- **Google** - one-tap or "Continue with Google". Uses your existing Google account, so there is no separate password to remember. Google One Tap can sign returning members in automatically.
- **Apple** - "Sign in with Apple". Uses your Apple ID and supports Apple's Hide My Email (private email relay).
- **Facebook** - "Continue with Facebook".
- **Email and password** - a traditional email and password login, with self-service password reset.

No matter which method you choose, an account is identified by its email address. Signing in with Google, Apple, or Facebook for the first time creates a 1club account (or links to an existing one with the same email) automatically. There is nothing to enable per member.

## How sign-in works

All member-facing apps (portal, marketplace, club website, and mobile) sign you in through a single secure sign-in page. Choose a provider or enter your email, authenticate once, and you are returned to the app you started from, already signed in. The mobile app uses the same page in a secure in-app browser, so a member's account and history are identical on web and mobile.

The admin dashboard has its own sign-in page with the same Google, Apple, and Facebook options plus email and password.

## Sessions and staying signed in

- After you sign in, 1club keeps you signed in as you keep using the app and refreshes your session automatically in the background - you don't re-enter credentials on every visit.
- Sessions are per device and per app. Signing out on one device does not sign you out on the others.
- Sessions are protected with secure, HTTP-only cookies, so your credentials are never exposed to page scripts.
- Signing out ends the session on that device. If you think an account is compromised, change the password (or the provider account) and sign out everywhere.

## Hide My Email with Apple

When you sign in with Apple, you can choose to hide your email. Apple then gives 1club a private relay address (ending in `@privaterelay.appleid.com`) instead of your real one.

You still receive everything as normal. 1club sends your account and notification emails - booking confirmations, receipts, reminders, and verification emails - to that relay address, and Apple forwards them to your real inbox. There's nothing to set up, and you can change the choice anytime in your Apple ID settings.

<Note>
  Emails to members who hid their Apple email are sent from a 1club address so
  Apple's relay accepts and forwards them, rather than from your club's own
  sending domain. Replies still reach your club.
</Note>

## Linking and switching methods

One email address is one 1club account, regardless of how you sign in. This means:

- If you first signed up with email and password, you can still choose "Continue with Google" or "Sign in with Apple" later, as long as the provider account uses the same email.
- If you signed up with Google, Apple, or Facebook and now want a password (for example, to sign in on a device without that provider), an org admin can start it for you from **Settings > Users** using the **Reset password** action. That sends you a link to set a password; after that, you can sign in with either method using the same email. See [Users](/settings/admin-users).
- Members can review their sign-in email on their profile. Staff can review and update theirs under [Profile](/settings/profile).

## Worked examples

### New staff member joining the admin

An admin invites the new hire from **Settings > Users**. The invitation email links them to sign-in. They click "Continue with Google" with their work Google account, and their admin access is ready - no password to create or share.

### Member who wants to keep their email private

A member books through the marketplace and chooses "Sign in with Apple", then picks "Hide My Email". They still get booking confirmations, receipts, and reminders in their real inbox, forwarded by Apple, without giving the club their real address.

### Social sign-up that later needs a password

A member created their account with Facebook, but wants to sign in on a shared kiosk that doesn't have Facebook. An admin uses **Reset password** on that user in **Settings > Users**; the member gets a link, sets a password, and can now sign in either way with the same email.

## Tips and best practices

- **Pick one primary method per person.** Any method works, but choosing one keeps sign-in predictable across devices.
- **Match the provider to the email.** Social sign-in only links to an existing account when the provider account uses the same email address.
- **Use Reset password to enable password login**, not to create a second account - the account stays the same; only the sign-in options grow.
- **Keep the sign-in email current.** Members and staff can review it on their profile; it is the key that ties every sign-in method to one account.

## Frequently asked questions

### Can members sign in with Google?

Yes. Members can use "Continue with Google" (including Google One Tap) on the member portal, the marketplace, your club website, and the mobile app.

### Does 1club support Sign in with Apple?

Yes. "Sign in with Apple" is available to members on every member-facing app and to staff on the admin dashboard, and it supports Apple's private email relay.

### Can I sign in with Facebook?

Yes. "Continue with Facebook" is available on every app - the member portal, the marketplace, your club website, the mobile app, and the admin dashboard.

### Can staff use Google, Apple, or Facebook to sign into the admin dashboard?

Yes. The admin dashboard offers Sign in with Google, Sign in with Apple, and Facebook login alongside email and password.

### Can I hide my email when I sign in with Apple?

Yes. Choose "Hide My Email" when Apple prompts you. 1club sends your notifications to the private relay address Apple provides, and Apple forwards them to your real inbox - so you still get booking confirmations, receipts, and reminders without sharing your real email.

### I signed up with Google - can I also use a password?

Yes. Ask an org admin to run **Reset password** for you from **Settings > Users**. You'll get a link to set a password, and after that you can sign in with either method using the same email.

### Do I need to create a separate account for the mobile app?

No. The mobile app uses the same sign-in page and the same account as the web apps. Sign in with any supported method and your memberships, bookings, and history are already there.
