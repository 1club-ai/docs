Access control settings cover two things: how members identify themselves at the door (QR code, access card, or PIN), and how strict your check-in flow is - whether staff can check in walk-ins or anonymous visitors, and whether payment is required. Identification and enrollment are set per club on the **Check-in & access control** page. The strictness of check-in is governed by your **booking policy**, not by a single toggle on this page.

## Overview

- **Identification methods (per club)** - choose which of QR code, access card, and PIN members can use, and let them enroll a method themselves.
- **Check-in modes (via booking policy)** - decide whether staff can check in a contact without a booking, check in an anonymous visitor with no contact, and whether payment is enforced at booking or at check-in.
- **Physical door hardware** - access grants and WebLock door sync are part of the native access control feature, documented separately.

If you're choosing how members get into your club, start with [Check-in models](/reference/check-in-models).

<Note>
There is no longer a single "Check-in mode" (Strict/Flexible) switch on the access control page. That legacy toggle was replaced by the booking-policy settings described below - **Allow walk-ins** and **Allow anonymous check-ins**.
</Note>

## Check-in modes (booking policy)

How strict your door is comes from your [booking policy](/operations/booking-policies) (**Settings → Operations → Booking policies**). The relevant toggles:

- **Allow walk-ins** - staff can check in an existing contact who has no booking. Off by default.
- **Allow anonymous check-ins** - staff can check in a visitor with no contact at all. Independent of walk-in check-ins. Off by default.
- **Allow anonymous bookings** - a booking can be created without selecting a contact. Off by default.
- **Check-in required** - a booking is marked no-show if the member isn't checked in by end of day.
- **Enforce payment** - **None**, **At booking**, or **At check-in**. This decides whether payment is collected before a check-in is recorded.

A "strict" club leaves the anonymous and walk-in toggles off and requires bookings. A "flexible" club turns on walk-ins and, if it accepts true guests, anonymous check-ins.

### How anonymous check-ins interact with Enforce payment

**Allow anonymous check-ins** and **Enforce payment** work together:

- **Anonymous off** - staff must pick a contact (with or without a booking, depending on the walk-in setting).
- **Anonymous on + Enforce payment: None** - anyone can be checked in, including a visitor with no account. The check-in is recorded with no contact link.
- **Anonymous on + Enforce payment: At check-in** - anonymous check-ins are still allowed, but only when the front desk also collects payment via an entrance method (external program or direct payment). A "no contact and no payment" check-in is rejected.

<Note>
Anonymous check-ins support only the **direct payment** and **external program** entrance methods. **Membership** entries are contact-scoped, so they can't back an anonymous check-in. Entrance methods are covered in [Check-in models](/reference/check-in-models).
</Note>

## Anonymous check-ins in practice

When anonymous check-ins are allowed, the front desk can record a check-in without picking a contact:

1. From the check-in flow, choose to record an anonymous check-in (a club must be selected).
2. Optionally note what they're entering for (class, area, instructor).
3. If your booking policy enforces payment at check-in, collect a direct payment or apply an external program now.
4. Save.

What gets recorded:

- A check-in row with no member/contact link.
- A reference to the class, area, or instructor (if selected).
- A payment transaction (if you collected one).

What does **not** happen:

- No membership usage is decremented (there's no member to attribute it to).
- The check-in won't appear in any single member's history.

Anonymous check-ins are best for guests, trial visitors, and one-off drop-ins. For repeat members, create a contact even if it's lightweight, so you can track usage and follow up.

## Member identification methods (per club)

Open **Settings → Check-in & access control**. Each club appears as its own block, and changes across clubs are saved together with the floating save alert at the bottom.

Turn on **Access control** for a club to enable **credential enrollment** - letting members register an access card, NFC device, or PIN themselves for physical entry, instead of staff registering one for them. When it's on, choose the **Enabled identification methods**:

- **QR Code** - members enroll a QR code and scan it at the door.
- **Access Card** - members enroll a physical access card (NFC/RFID).
- **PIN Code** - members enroll a numeric PIN to type at the door.

All three are enabled by default; you can turn off any you don't accept. When a method is off, members can't enroll it themselves, though staff can still register one manually.

At the door, 1Club identifies the member in this order: **PIN**, then **access card**, then **QR code**. A PIN is matched on the full 6-digit code, and a kiosk can accept just the last 4 digits as a shortcut.

<Note>
The **Check-in & access control** page also manages **access grants** and WebLock door-hardware sync - the native access control feature that drives physical locks. That side is documented separately; see [Access control settings](/settings/access-control-settings).
</Note>

## Worked examples

### Kiosk-style walk-in studio

A small studio with lots of trial visitors that wants minimal friction at the door.

- **Booking policy** - **Allow walk-ins** on, **Allow anonymous check-ins** on, **Enforce payment** set to **At check-in** so the system still collects from anonymous visitors.
- **Identification** - **Access control** on with **QR Code** enabled, so returning members self-serve.

### Strict members-only class gym

A class-based gym where every member has an account and bookings are mandatory.

- **Booking policy** - **Allow walk-ins** and **Allow anonymous check-ins** off, **Check-in required** on, **Enforce payment** set to **At booking**.
- **Identification** - **Access control** on with all three methods enabled.

### Multi-club organization

A flagship club can run a strict booking-based policy while a community-run satellite allows walk-ins. Scope different booking policies per club (see [Booking policies](/operations/booking-policies)), and set identification methods independently for each club on the access control page.

## Tips & best practices

- **Match your booking policy to your front-desk culture.** If staff routinely handle walk-ins, turn on **Allow walk-ins**. If your model depends on bookings, leave it off to prevent accidental free entries.
- **Only allow anonymous check-ins if you truly accept accountless visitors.** They aren't attributable to any member, so revenue and attendance reporting won't tie back to a person.
- **Collect payment on anonymous entries.** Pair **Allow anonymous check-ins** with **Enforce payment: At check-in** so drop-ins still pay.
- **Set identification methods per club.** A flagship with turnstiles might use access cards, while a studio uses QR only.

## Troubleshooting

**Issue**: I turned on walk-ins but staff still can't check someone in without payment.
**Cause / fix**: The booking policy that applies at this club has **Enforce payment** set to **At check-in**, so an entrance method (external program or direct payment) is required. Collect payment, or set Enforce payment to **None**.

**Issue**: Members can't enroll their QR code themselves.
**Cause / fix**: Confirm **Access control** is on for that club and **QR Code** is in the enabled identification methods.

**Issue**: A check-in shows up with no member attached.
**Cause / fix**: That's an anonymous check-in. They only appear when **Allow anonymous check-ins** is on for the applicable booking policy. Turn it off if you didn't intend to accept them.

**Issue**: I can't find the old "Check-in mode" toggle.
**Cause / fix**: It was removed. Use the booking-policy toggles **Allow walk-ins** and **Allow anonymous check-ins** instead.
