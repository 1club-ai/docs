Notification settings control which categories of notifications you personally receive and on which channels they reach you. A **notification** is the system's record of something that happened, addressed to a person ("a member booked a court", "a renewal payment failed"). It is delivered through one or more **channels** - in-app, push, email, or SMS - and which channels fire for you, per category, is exactly what this page governs.

These preferences are per user: each staff member sets their own. They do not change what the system generates, only how it reaches you.

## Overview

- **Per-category toggles** - Turn each notification category on or off for yourself.
- **Per-category channels** - For an enabled category, choose which channels deliver it.
- **Only relevant categories shown** - You see the categories you can actually receive, based on your role and whether you have an instructor profile.
- **Opt-in vs on-by-default** - Most categories are on by default; booking-activity categories start off until you opt in.
- **Staff routing** - Member-action alerts (new booking, new signup, new message) route to specific roles and to the relevant instructors automatically.

## Managing your preferences

Open **Settings > My notifications** (in the **Me** category of Settings). Each row is one category with:

1. **The category label** on the left.
2. **A switch** to enable or disable that category for you.
3. **A Channels selector** (shown only when the category is enabled) where you pick the delivery channels.

Changes save immediately when you toggle a switch or change channels - there is no separate save button. A confirmation toast appears on success.

## Notification categories

The categories fall into two groups: your own personal activity, and staff alerts about what members do.

### Personal categories (always available)

These concern your own account and are offered to every staff member:

| Category | Label | What it covers |
| --- | --- | --- |
| `message` | My inbox | New messages sent to you. |
| `task` | My tasks | Task-related notifications assigned to you. |
| `booking` | My bookings | Activity on your own bookings. |
| `billing` | My billing | Your own billing notifications. |

### Staff-alert categories (shown when relevant to you)

These notify staff about member actions. You only see a category if it can actually route to you (see [Which categories you see](#which-categories-you-see)):

| Category | Label | Fires when | Routes to |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| `user_booking` | User bookings | A member books or cancels | Admins, managers |
| `class_instructor` | Class bookings | A member books or cancels a class | The class's instructors |
| `user_membership` | User memberships | A member buys a membership | Admins, managers |
| `user_message` | User messages | A member sends an inbound message | Admins, managers, front desk, instructors |
| `user_review` | User reviews | A member leaves a review | Admins and managers; the reviewed session's instructor |
| `user_signup` | User signups | A new member signs up themselves | Admins, managers |

Renewal-payment-failure alerts route to admins and managers and are delivered under your **My billing** category.

<Note>
Per-event booking alerts for a specific event are gated by that event's own "notify staff" setting, not by a toggle here. When an organizer turns that on, the alert always reaches admins and managers regardless of their personal **User bookings** preference, so it does not appear as a separate switch on this page.
</Note>

### Which categories you see

The list is filtered to what you can receive:

- **Personal categories** (My inbox, My tasks, My bookings, My billing) always appear.
- **Role-based staff categories** appear when your role is in the alert's recipient roles. An admin or manager sees User bookings, User memberships, User signups, and so on; front desk sees User messages.
- **Instructor categories** (Class bookings, and instructor reviews) appear when you have an instructor profile, so you're notified about your own classes and sessions.

Owners are treated as admins for relevance, so they see every category.

## Channels

A notification can be delivered on any of these channels:

- **In-app** - Appears in your notification center inside 1Club. Turning this off for a category removes those notifications from the notification center entirely.
- **Push** - The mobile-app nudge. Push and in-app are two channels of the same notification and share its read state, not two separate notifications.
- **Email** - Sent through SendGrid, using your organization's branding.
- **SMS** - A text message, sent through Twilio, used for staff alerts where a mobile number is on file.

In this settings screen you select **In-App** and **Email** per category. Push is governed by your mobile app, and SMS is used by the platform for staff alerts where configured; both ride the same per-category preferences model.

### How channels resolve

For each category, the effective channels are the ones you selected, intersected with the channels that category actually supports - you never receive a channel you didn't ask for. Beyond that:

- **Category off** - You receive nothing for that category, on any channel.
- **On-by-default categories with no saved preference** - You receive them on their default channels (In-App and Email).
- **Opt-in categories** (User bookings and My bookings) - You receive nothing until you enable them; they start off by design to avoid noise from high-volume booking activity.

<Note>
Deleted, invited (invitation not yet accepted), and inactive accounts are never notified on any channel, regardless of preferences. This is a structural safeguard, not something you configure.
</Note>

## Staff notification routing

Staff alerts about member actions are resolved and delivered automatically. Understanding the routing helps you set expectations for your team:

- **By role** - Alerts like New booking, New membership, and New signup go to every active user whose role matches (admins and managers for most; front desk and instructors are also included for inbound messages).
- **By instructor** - Class-booking and instructor-review alerts resolve to the instructors of the specific class or session involved: the directly assigned instructor for one-to-one sessions, plus the class's instructors for class bookings. Only active instructors with an active linked account are reachable.
- **Deduplicated** - If one person matches more than one rule for the same event (for example an admin who also teaches the booked class), they receive a single notification, not one per rule.
- **Per-recipient channels** - Each recipient's own notification preferences decide which channels fire for them. One person may get a new booking in-app while another gets it by email.

Delivery per channel is independent: an email failure doesn't suppress the in-app notification, and vice versa.

## Worked examples

### Yoga studio owner who wants signal, not noise

You run a busy pilates and yoga studio and don't want a ping for every class booking.

- Leave **User bookings** off (it starts off anyway).
- Keep **User memberships** and **User signups** on with **In-App** and **Email** so you see new members and new revenue.
- Keep **My inbox** on so member replies reach you.

### Instructor at a climbing gym

You teach classes and want to know about your own sessions only.

- **Class bookings** appears because you have an instructor profile - keep it on to be told when someone books your class.
- **User reviews** appears too - keep it on to see reviews of your sessions.
- You won't see admin-only categories like User signups, because they never route to you.

### Front desk at a racket-sports club

You triage member enquiries at reception.

- **User messages** appears for your role - keep it on with **In-App** so inbound member emails and messages surface immediately at the desk.
- Turn on **User bookings** if reception also tracks new court bookings; leave it off if that's noise for you.

### Studio manager who prefers email digests over in-app

You'd rather everything arrive by email.

- For each staff category you care about, open its **Channels** selector and choose **Email** only, removing **In-App**.
- Those categories then skip the notification center and land in your inbox instead.

## Tips & best practices

- **Start from the defaults.** Most categories are on with In-App and Email out of the box. Turn things off rather than trying to enable everything.
- **Opt in to booking alerts deliberately.** User bookings and My bookings are off by design; enable them only if you actually want per-booking pings.
- **Use channels to match how you work.** Front-desk staff often prefer In-App; owners who live in email can drop In-App for staff categories.
- **Remember routing is role-based.** If a colleague isn't getting an alert, check their role and instructor profile before assuming a preference is wrong - some categories simply never route to some roles.

## Troubleshooting

**Issue**: I'm not receiving a category I expect.

**Cause / fix**: Either the category is toggled off, or it's an opt-in category (User bookings, My bookings) you haven't enabled. Turn the switch on and confirm at least one channel is selected.

**Issue**: A category I want isn't even listed on the page.

**Cause / fix**: The list only shows categories that can route to you. Admin-only categories won't appear for a front-desk or instructor account, and instructor categories require an instructor profile. Adjust the person's role or instructor profile if they genuinely need it.

**Issue**: I enabled a category but still get nothing.

**Cause / fix**: Check the **Channels** selector for that row - if no channels are selected the category effectively delivers nothing. Also confirm the account isn't invited-but-not-accepted or inactive, which never receive notifications.

**Issue**: A staff member gets duplicate alerts for the same booking.

**Cause / fix**: They shouldn't - matching multiple routing rules for one event is deduplicated to a single notification. If you see genuine duplicates, confirm they aren't two different categories (for example a class instructor also being an admin who enabled User bookings), which are distinct notifications by design.

**Issue**: Turning off In-App didn't stop the email (or vice versa).

**Cause / fix**: Channels are independent. Removing In-App only stops the notification-center entry; you must also remove Email from that category's channels to stop the email.
