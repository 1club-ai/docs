**Settings > Profile** is your personal account page. It is the one settings screen every admin user can open regardless of role, because everything here is about you, not the organization. You reach it from the account menu at the bottom of the sidebar or directly under the **My account** area of Settings.

## Overview

From your profile you can:

- **Update your identity** - profile photo, first name, and last name shown across the admin.
- **Manage your sign-in email** - and resend a verification email if it is not yet verified.
- **Add and verify a phone number** - used for SMS notifications.
- **Change your password** - with your current password.
- **Switch language and theme** - from the account menu in the sidebar (not the profile form itself).

You cannot change your own role here. Roles are managed by an admin under [Users](/settings/admin-users).

## What you can edit

The profile form has one section, **Profile information**, with a floating save bar that appears as soon as you change anything.

- **Profile image** - Upload from your device. Uploaded files are stored under `1club/users`. Your avatar appears next to your name in user lists and the account menu.
- **First name**, **Last name** - Your display name in user lists and the account menu.
- **Email** - Your sign-in email. A chip shows whether it is verified. If it is not, a warning icon appears with a send action - click it to trigger a fresh verification email.
- **Phone** - Enter in international format. A chip shows whether the number is verified. If it is not, click the send action to open the verification dialog and confirm the number with a code. The verified chip tracks the number currently in the field, so an edited-but-unsaved number reads as unverified until you verify and save it.

### How saving works

Edit any field and a floating **Save** bar appears at the bottom of the page. Click it to commit. Your name, email, phone, and profile image sync across every browser and device you sign in from.

## Changing your password

Click **Change password** in the top right to open the dialog. It asks for your current password, a new password, and a confirmation.

- The new password must meet the listed password requirements (at least 8 characters).
- Changing your password revokes your other active sessions for security, so you stay signed in on this device but are signed out elsewhere.
- If you signed in with Google, Apple, or Facebook and never set a local password, you cannot use this dialog because it requires your current password. Ask an admin to send you a password reset from [Users](/settings/admin-users) instead. See [Signing in](/reference/sign-in) for how sign-in methods link to one account.

## Language and theme

Language and theme are not on the profile form. You set them from the account menu at the bottom of the sidebar.

- **Language** - Choose English, French, Bulgarian, or Spanish. The change applies immediately and is saved to your account, so it follows you on your next sign-in.
- **Theme** - Choose **Light**, **Dark**, or **System** (follows your operating system). Theme is saved per browser, so it does not leave the local machine.

## Notifications

Which events email, SMS, or push to you is configured on a separate **Notifications** page under **My account**, not on the profile form.

## Worked examples

### Front desk staff at a padel club enabling SMS alerts

A receptionist wants to receive SMS alerts for new bookings. On their profile they enter a phone number in international format, click the send action to verify it with the code they receive, and save. Their number now shows the verified chip and can receive SMS notifications.

### Instructor switching the admin to French

A jiu-jitsu coach prefers French. They open the account menu in the sidebar, pick **Français** under language, and the admin switches immediately. The choice is saved to their account, so it stays French the next time they sign in on any device.

## Tips and best practices

- **Verify your email and phone.** Unverified contact details can block or delay notifications and some sign-in flows.
- **Use a real profile photo.** It makes your entries easier to recognise in shared user lists and activity.
- **Keep your recovery method current.** If you rely on a social sign-in, make sure the linked email is one you control, since password resets go there.
- **Save before you leave.** The floating save bar only commits when you click it - navigating away discards unsaved edits.

## Troubleshooting

**Issue**: The email verified chip stays as a warning after I clicked resend.

**Solution**: The verification email was sent, but the chip only clears once you open the email and complete verification. Check your inbox and spam folder, then reload.

**Issue**: My phone shows as unverified even though I verified it before.

**Solution**: The verified chip tracks the number currently in the field. If you edited the number, verify the new one. If it matches your saved number and still reads unverified, save the profile and reload.

**Issue**: The Change password dialog rejects me or I never set a password.

**Solution**: The dialog needs your current password. If you only ever signed in with Google, Apple, or Facebook, you have no local password to enter. Ask an admin to send a password reset from [Users](/settings/admin-users), then set a password from the reset link.

**Issue**: I want to change my own role.

**Solution**: You cannot change your own role from your profile. Ask an admin to update it in [Users](/settings/admin-users). What you can see and do is set by your role - see [Roles and permissions](/reference/roles-permissions).
